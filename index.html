What is ALS?

  • stands for Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis
  • commonly known as Lou Gehrig's Disease
    • named for baseball player who was diagnosed with ALS
  • a neurodegenerative disease that attacks nerve cells in the brain and the spinal cord
"What Is ALS?" The ALS Association. The ALS Association, n.d. Web. 11 Feb. 2015. Lou Gehrig. Digital image. Wikipedia. N.p., n.d. Web. 2 Mar. 2015.

What is ALS? (continued)

  • motor neurons, which connect the brain to muscles, are damaged by ALS and eventually die
  • as motor neurons die, the brain loses the ability to control muscles
  • when muscles stop receiving signals from the neurons, they begin to atrophy
  • involuntary muscles such as the heart are not affected
  • causes pain in the back, loss of muscle, and shortness of breath
"What Is ALS?" The ALS Association. The ALS Association, n.d. Web. 11 Feb. 2015. Murray, Christine A. Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis: New Research. New York: Nova Biomedical, 2006. Print.

What is ALS? (further continued)

  • SIGMAR1 mutation is located on chromosome 9 at positon 13.3
  • mutation changes E to Q at the 102nd amino acid, which reduces the viability of the motor neurons
  • popularized through the ALS ice bucket challenge (that's Mr. Dwyer down there!)
"SIGMAR1 Gene." Genetics Home Reference. N.p., n.d. Web. 02 Mar. 2015.
